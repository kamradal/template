From the Lens To Life

Growing up, many different pieces of technology influenced my view of life. From the way I viewed the world, to the way I learned about it. Out of all the different technologies that I have encountered, the one device to really grab my interest was a basic, point-and-shoot, digital camera. It changed everything about me. I was able to see the world in a way that I had never been able to experience before. This simple camera led me to finding a path in life that I had never thought about before I came across this device.

I moved into my mother’s fiancé’s house my sophomore year of high school. He had a digital camera that he would let me borrow on occasion. I decided to use the camera to experiment with outdoor photography. I would find myself wandering our neighborhood for hours, seeing the world in a whole new, artistic way. I would disappear for hours photographing the fascinating world around me. My particular interest was in macro photography, in which I would take close-ups of flowers, bugs, and various other interesting things I would find. My favorite thing to do was to wait for after a storm to photograph the flowers around the house. The way the droplets sparkle on the bright petals is still one of the most beautiful things I have ever witnessed.

Once my love for photography was noticed, I was given my own point-and-shoot camera for Christmas. It was a bright blue and extremely shiny. I was very excited to receive my own camera and to be able to create beautiful photography whenever I wanted. The majority of my Christmas was spent outside, using my new camera. I remember capturing the gentle grace of the glittering snow around me. I captured the way the shadows fell over the icy environment and the shine of the hanging icicles.

The summer after I received my new camera, I spent every day outside taking more pictures than I could practically deal with. I would come home with five hundred different shots to upload to the computer. I had to spend a lot of time picking which ones to keep and which ones to toss. I would toy with the camera settings to figure out which ones worked with different subjects and different lighting. I taught myself everything that I could possible know about my little camera.

Then, I got the best gift I have ever received to this very day; a Canon EOS Digital Rebel XT. It was a “just because” gift from my grandfather. He bought the camera for himself but decided he didn’t like it (for some reason that I cannot imagine) and decided to give it to me since I enjoyed photography so much. I remember coming home and finding a suitcase sitting on my bed. I opened up the metal latches to discover the camera, a different lens, a filter, lens covers, an extra battery, a charger, and a connection wire. I was completely stunned. I ran upstairs to ask my mom what it was for. She explained that it was all mine and that grandpa had given it to me because he knew I had a talent for photography. It took me a few minutes to figure out the functions of this new, complex piece of equipment, but soon enough I was producing greater photography than I ever had before. The camera continues to produce such great quality photos that I could only dream of taking with the basic point-and-shoots. The Canon opened the door to an even greater expansion of my photo-artistic abilities. I moved into using people as subjects for my photography. I have done four senior photo sessions since I received my Canon. Like the other camera, I had taught myself the many functions and setting that the camera has to offer. Even to this day, I am still learning new things about this special piece of technology. Since my camera was of higher quality, I didn’t need to take as many shots to get the perfect one. The memory on my hard-drive continues to be thankful for that.

Eventually, I broadened my skills with work in Adobe Photoshop as well. I can add gradients, filters, and enhance different aspects of my photography to make the images pop. I eventually was able to do things like changing hair color or making people look like they were shattering. With a little help from Photoshop, my photography was reaching new heights in artistic beauty.

That’s when I started to think about making photography a career. My love of photography, mixed with a love of writing and adventure, led me to pick a major in journalism. This major would allow me to use all of my favorite things in one career path. I would be able to travel the world; seeing it with my eyes and through a lens. In fact, if I became good enough, others would also be able to see what I saw through my work. My dream is to do a photojournalistic piece for National Geographic. All I ask for is one piece: big or small. I know that when I accomplish my goals and dreams, that I’ll be able to look back one day and have the little, point-and shoot camera to thank for my success and happiness.
